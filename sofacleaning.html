Sofa Cleaning Services

Our professional sofa cleaning service is designed to restore the freshness and beauty of your upholstered furniture. We use specialized equipment and fabric-safe, eco-friendly solutions to remove dust, stains, odors, and allergens that accumulate deep within the fabric.

Whether it's fabric, leather, or suede, we carefully assess the material and apply the most suitable cleaning method to ensure a deep clean without damaging the upholstery. The result is a cleaner, healthier, and more inviting living space with sofas that look and feel like new.

What the Benifits?

. It removes deep-seated dust, dirt, stains, and allergens that regular surface cleaning can’t reach, making your living space healthier and more comfortable. By eliminating bacteria and odors, it improves indoor air quality and helps maintain a fresh, welcoming environment. Regular sofa cleaning also extends the life of your furniture by preventing fabric damage and color fading. Whether you have kids, pets, or simply frequent use, a deep sofa clean keeps your upholstery looking new and feeling fresh.

Service Quality

We use advanced cleaning equipment and safe, fabric-specific solutions to gently yet effectively remove stains, dust, and odors from all types of upholstery. Our trained professionals assess the material before treatment to ensure the right method is used—whether steam cleaning, dry cleaning, or foam-based techniques. We prioritize care, hygiene, and long-lasting results, leaving your sofa clean, refreshed, and protected without any damage to the fabric or structure.

Freequently Asked Question

  • What types of sofas do you clean?

    We clean all types of sofas, including fabric, leather, suede, microfiber, and sectional sofas.

  • How often should I get my sofa professionally cleaned?

    It’s recommended to have your sofa professionally cleaned every 6 to 12 months, or more frequently if you have kids or pets.

  • How long does the sofa take to dry after cleaning?

    Drying time varies based on the fabric and method used, but typically takes between 4 to 6 hours.
